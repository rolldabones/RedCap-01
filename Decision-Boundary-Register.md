## Design Checklist

Each boundary should:

- Support a strategic objective
- Be understandable by decision-makers
- Distinguish acceptable from unacceptable conditions
- Be supported by observable indicators where possible
- Link directly to one or more triggers
- Specify the action expected when exceeded

---

## Questions

1. Can leadership apply this boundary in less than one minute?
2. Would two independent reviewers interpret it consistently?
3. Does crossing the boundary change a decision?
4. Is ownership explicit?
5. Is review cadence defined?

---

## Notes

Decision boundaries should be reviewed whenever objectives, assumptions or strategy materially change.

Risk appetite becomes operational only when translated into usable decision boundaries.
