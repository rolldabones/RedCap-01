# RedCap-01 Example Assessment

> This fictional example illustrates the RedCap-01 method. It is not based on any real organization.

---

# Executive Summary

**Objective**

Expand into a new regional market while maintaining profitability and service quality.

**Governing Question**

Does the organization's ERM improve the decisions that determine whether this objective is achieved?

**Overall Assessment**

**Developing**

The organization has a documented ERM program and executive reporting, but most risk artifacts are produced after strategic decisions have been made. Risk appetite is expressed in general terms and is not consistently translated into operational decision boundaries.

---

# 1. Objective

Increase regional revenue by 20% within 24 months through market expansion.

**Owner:** Chief Executive Officer

**Success Measures**

- Revenue growth
- Customer retention
- Gross margin
- Time to market

---

# 2. Critical Decisions

| Decision | ERM Influence | Observation |
|---|---|---|
| Enter market | Partial | Risk reviewed after investment recommendation. |
| Select local partner | Strong | Vendor due diligence influenced selection. |
| Allocate capital | Limited | Financial scenarios prepared, but not linked to risk appetite. |
| Launch timing | Limited | No predefined triggers for delay or acceleration. |

---

# 3. Assumptions

| Assumption | Status |
|---|---|
| Regulatory approvals obtained on schedule | Moderate confidence |
| Local partner performs as expected | Moderate confidence |
| Supply chain capacity available | High confidence |

No formal process exists to monitor assumption drift.

---

# 4. Uncertainty

Three scenarios were identified.

**Expected**

Regulatory approval within six months.

**Downside**

Approval delayed by twelve months, increasing cost and reducing projected return.

**Upside**

Accelerated approval enables earlier launch and first-mover advantage.

Discussion focused on likelihood scores rather than management actions under each scenario.

---

# 5. Decision Boundaries

Current risk appetite states that the organization has a "moderate appetite for expansion."

No measurable definition exists.

Recommended translation:

| Boundary | Threshold | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Capital overrun | >10% | Executive review |
| Regulatory delay | >90 days | Reassess launch schedule |
| Partner performance | SLA below agreed minimum | Activate contingency partner |

---

# 6. Triggers

| Trigger | Response |
|---|---|
| Regulatory milestone missed | Executive review within five business days |
| Major supplier failure | Activate alternate supplier |
| Budget variance exceeds threshold | Pause discretionary spending and reassess business case |

---

# 7. Operating Rhythm

ERM appears in quarterly reporting but not consistently during:

- investment committee meetings
- transformation governance
- monthly performance reviews

Opportunity exists to embed risk discussions earlier.

---

# 8. Evidence of Value

Positive examples:

- Vendor risk assessment prevented selection of a higher-risk partner.
- Cybersecurity review identified integration weaknesses before implementation.

Evidence was limited that ERM materially influenced the initial market-entry decision.

---

# Strengths

- Clear strategic objective
- Executive sponsorship
- Good vendor risk practices
- Regular board reporting

# Gaps

- Objectives and risks are weakly connected.
- Risk appetite is not operationalized.
- Triggers are informal.
- Assumptions are not actively monitored.

# Recommendations

## Immediate

- Define measurable decision boundaries.
- Assign ownership for assumptions.
- Establish three objective-specific triggers.

## Next 90 Days

- Embed ERM into investment committee agendas.
- Replace heat-map discussion with scenario and trigger discussion.
- Record decisions demonstrably influenced by ERM.

## Longer Term

- Measure ERM by decisions improved rather than artifacts produced.
- Introduce periodic post-decision learning reviews.

# Conclusion

The organization has the foundations of an effective ERM capability but has not yet demonstrated that ERM consistently improves the decisions most critical to achieving strategic objectives.

RedCap-01 therefore concludes that the ERM program is **Developing**, with the highest priorities being stronger objective linkage, operational decision boundaries and trigger-based governance.

**Final Liability rests with the Human.**
